What is Culture & Society?

Culture refers to the beliefs, values, traditions, and behaviors shared by a group of people. Society includes the structured relationships and systems that organize human interaction. In the Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) strand, we study both how culture influences people and how societies evolve over time.

Key Themes in HUMSS Exploration

Cultural Identity

How traditions, language, and heritage shape who we are and how we see others.

Social Systems

How communities organize roles, rules, and institutions to function and grow.

Change & Continuity

Examining how societies change and what traditions remain through generations.

Communication & Expression

The role of language, media, and stories in reflecting and shaping society.

Real World Case Studies

Examples of cultural and societal change include the influence of literature and art on social movements, the role of festivals and rituals in community identity, and how language evolves as people interact and connect across different regions.

About the HUMSS Strand

The Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) strand helps students develop skills in analysis, communication, and critical thinking while exploring human behavior, culture, society, and politics. In this strand, students research topics, interpret traditions, and learn to explain how human communities interact and change. 2
